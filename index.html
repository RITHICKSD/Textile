Warping the Loom

Warping is the critical first stage. High-tensile cotton cords are wound meticulously onto the structural steel or wood frame loom, creating the vertical foundation of the tapestry. Equal tension across all warp cords is paramount to prevent distortions in the final wall hanging.

Our Story

Honoring the Heritage of
Warp & Weft

Established in 2012, AuraWeave Studio began with a single wooden frame loom and a vision to bring tactile warmth back into contemporary residential architecture.

Studio Milestones

2012

First Warp Thrown

Founded by artist Clara Gable in a small barn studio. We sold locally and held basic frame loom classes.

2016

Dye Garden Expansion

Acquired adjacent greenhouses to cultivate native madder root, marigolds, and weld for local yarn botanical dyeing projects.

2021

Architectural Focus

Launched corporate commission arm, weaving acoustic-panel hangings for international lobbies and hotel complexes.

Foundations

Our Weaving Philosophy

Preservation of Craft

We utilize purely mechanical timber looms, resisting automation to ensure every row honors manual, artistic choice.

100% Ethical Sourcing

We trace every fiber back to shepherds and farmers, ensuring safe working conditions and fair trade pay.

Tactile Exploration

We experiment constantly with unusual fibers—metallic threads, cedar bark, dried seagrass—to redefine boundaries.
